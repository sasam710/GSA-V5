No guesswork

From enquiry to departure.

We've run this process hundreds of times across dozens of trips. Here's exactly what happens after you reach out.

01

You reach out

Fill in our brief enquiry form or send us an email. No commitment, no pressure. Just tell us you're interested and when you'd be available for a 20-minute call.

We respond to every enquiry within 24 hours. Usually same day.

02

The qualification call

A 20-minute call with a member of our family. We want to understand what you're hoping to get out of the trip, answer any questions you have, and make sure the group dynamic is right. Small groups work best when everyone's there for the right reasons.

This is also where we walk through pricing in full — no number withheld, no surprise fees at checkout.

03

Reserve your place

A deposit holds your spot in the group. November 2026 departures have limited places — once the group is confirmed, we close bookings.

After your deposit: we send the full itinerary document, flight guidance for getting yourself to Cape Town, a pre-trip reading list, and a packing guide. You're in good hands.

04

Pre-departure care

A group call 6–8 weeks before departure. A final logistics email 2 weeks out. Emergency contact numbers for every moment of the trip. We're reachable 24 hours a day from the moment you land.

05

You just show up

Your airport transfer is waiting. Your room is ready. Your first dinner is booked. You don't organise anything — not a restaurant reservation, not a museum ticket, not a taxi. That's what we're here for.

Common questions

The questions everyone asks.

Is South Africa safe to visit?

Cape Town is a major international destination with millions of visitors each year. We stay in the V&A Waterfront — one of the most well-maintained and safe areas in the city. All our transfers are private and vetted. We'll give you a thorough safety briefing before arrival and our team is on-call throughout the trip. The honest answer is that millions of people visit Cape Town every year and have extraordinary experiences. Common sense and good guidance go a long way.

How far is the flight?

From Los Angeles: approximately 19–21 hours with one connection (usually via Johannesburg or London). It's a long day of travel — we won't pretend otherwise. But every person who's made the journey tells us it was worth it before Day 3 is out. We provide full flight guidance as part of your booking.

Do I need vaccinations?

Cape Town and the Western Cape are low-risk for most tropical diseases. No malaria prophylaxis is required — our safari at Aquila is specifically malaria-free. We recommend speaking with your doctor about your personal health history. Standard travel vaccinations are usually all that's needed.

What's the group like?

Our first departure is drawn primarily from our network in Southern California — people who've known our family for years and wanted to travel with us. We keep groups to 10–20 people for a reason: it's small enough to feel personal, large enough to have a real group dynamic. We do a qualification call with every participant to make sure the fit is right.

What if I need to cancel?

We strongly recommend comprehensive travel insurance for all participants — we'll point you to the right providers as part of your booking. Our cancellation policy is provided in full at the time of deposit. We work with you as much as possible.
